# dc-revenue
A guide to DC's revenue sources. 

Updated Tuesday, July 14 2026


## Income and franchise taxes

### Individual income tax 

Details:

* Tax on DC residents' income. 
* Reported on Form D-40<sup>[1]</sup>
* Previously there was another form called the D-40EZ, which people with simpler taxes could file instead of the full D-40, but that was discontinued in 2019.<sup>[2]</sup>
* If a person doesn't have automatic payroll witholding (e.g. because they're self-employed), they have to submit estimated payments using the D-40ES form. These are due quarterly.<sup>[3]</sup>

[read more details](###More-details-on-the-individual-income-tax)

Who doesn't have to file the D-40 or pay this tax:

* Low-income earners whose gross income is below the current standard deduction, unless they want to claim refundable credits, like the DC EITC.
* Workers who commute into DC but live in another state are exempt from DC income tax.
* Some diplomats. (Something like 3% of DC's population is foreign diplomats, or roughly 22,000 of DC's ~700k residents in 2024)<sup>[4]</sup>

## Business-related taxes 

### Corporate franchise tax

Details:

* "Paid by businesses structured as corporations (including S corporations that are not subject to federal corporate income tax)" (DC FPI). EX: C corps, S corps, LLCs with C corp elections. (S corps can have a max of 100 shareholders).
* Reported on Form D-20.
* 8.25% of profits earned in DC.
* There's a minimum amount the subject organizations have to pay: 
    * $250 if: $12k < gross reciepts < $1M 
    * $1k  if: gross reciepts > $1M
* The tax is on net income (earnings - costs). For out-of-state entities, this is total income, pro-rated to the portion of total income earned in DC (e.g. based on DC sales vs total sales).
* 4-times-a-year estimated reporting is required if the tax liability is > $1,000.

Who doesn't have to file the D-20 or pay this tax:

* Sole proprietorships, or businesses that are just one person, in which the owner and the business are the same legal entity. These people just pay their taxes on the D-40, as individual income tax.
* Entities that had gross reciepts < $12k
* Non-profits
* Unincorporated businesses (see next section)

### Unincorporated business franchise tax

Details:

* Taxes non-corporation businesses, like LLCs, partnerships, and a sole proprietorships.
* Reported on Form D-30.
* 8.25% of profits earned in DC; minimums work the same way as the CFT (above). This means some smaller landlords pay the tax.
* 4-times-a-year estimated reporting is required if the tax liability is > $1,000.
* "A 30% salary allowance for owners and a $5,000 exemption are deductible from net income to arrive at taxable income."<sup>[5]</sup>


Who doesn't have to file the D-30 or pay this tax:

* Non-profits
* "A business is exempt if more than 80% of gross income is derived from personal services rendered by the members of the entity and capital is not a material income-producing factor. A trade, business or professional organization that by law, customs or ethics cannot be incorporated is exempt."<sup>[6]</sup> 
    * This means some large law and consulting firms don't pay this tax.

## Employment-related

Employer withholding — reported on the FR-900 series (FR-900Q quarterly, FR-900A annual, FR-900P payment voucher). OTR
Unemployment insurance tax — administered by the Department of Employment Services (DOES), not OTR; employers register through DOES and file quarterly wage reports (Form UC-30) through DOES's ESSP portal. OTR
Paid Family Leave tax — employer payroll tax, also administered by DOES alongside UI quarterly reporting.

Sales, use, and excise

Sales and use tax — applies to the sale, lease, or rental of tangible personal property and certain services. Reported on the FR-800 series: FR-800A (annual, liability ≤$200/period), FR-800Q (quarterly), FR-800M (monthly), plus FR-800SE for special events and FR-800V for street/mobile food vendors. Higher rates apply to restaurant meals, alcohol, hotel stays, and parking, but those all flow through the same FR-800 return. OTR + 2
Tobacco products excise tax — Form FR-1000Q. Office of Attorney General
Motor fuel tax — wholesaler-level tax, reported on FR-400M (monthly).
Alcoholic beverage taxes — off-premises alcohol is taxed at a higher sales tax rate through the FR-800; there's also a gallonage tax on wholesalers reported to OTR.
Motor vehicle excise tax — paid at titling through the DC DMV, not via an OTR form.

Property-related

Real property tax — no "return" for most owners; it's billed semiannually by OTR based on assessments. Related filings include the Income and Expense Report (mandatory for many income-producing properties, now online-only), the ASD-100 (Homestead deduction application), and ASD-105 (cancellation). Vacant/blighted classification (higher Class 3/4 rates) runs through the Department of Buildings. OTR
Personal property tax — on business tangible personal property. Form FP-31, filed annually by all businesses; tax is owed only if personal property value exceeds $225,000, but the filing obligation exists regardless. LLC University®
Deed recordation and transfer taxes — paid when real estate changes hands, filed with the Recorder of Deeds on Form FP-7/C.

Estate

Estate tax — estates passing entirely to a spouse or 501(c) organization (resulting in $0 taxable estate) file the D-76EZ; all other estates file the D-76, now filed via MyTax. OTR

Industry-specific assessments and fees (registered via the FR-500 business registration)
The Ballpark Fee (FR-1500), and Department of Health Care Finance assessments: FR-1600M nursing facility assessment, FR-1700Q ICF-IID assessment, FR-1800 hospital revenue assessment, FR-1900Q hospital outpatient assessment, and FR-2000Q Medicaid inpatient/outpatient assessment. There are also insurance premium taxes (administered by DISB) and public utility/toll telecommunication gross receipts taxes (FR-27 series). Office of Attorney General
One more oddity: DC has an individual health insurance mandate — residents without minimum essential coverage pay a penalty of 2.5% of taxable income, with exemptions claimed on Schedule HSR, attached to the D-40. Washingtondctaxattorney
The single best consolidated reference is OTR's forms hub, which collects individual income, business, property-related, and specialized tax forms in one place, and the FR-500 business registration form is effectively a map of every business tax type DC imposes. OTR







## Nuts and bolts

### More details on the individual income tax

| If the taxable income is:             | The tax is:                                          |
|---------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| Not over $10,000                      | 4% of the taxable income.                            |
| Over $10,000 but not over $40,000     | $400, plus 6% of the excess over $10,000.            |
| Over $40,000 but not over $60,000     | $2,200, plus 6.5% of the excess over $40,000.        |
| Over $60,000 but not over $250,000    | $3,500, plus 8.5% of the excess over $60,000.        |
| Over $250,000 but not over $500,000   | $19,650, plus 9.25% of the excess over $250,000.     |
| Over $500,000 but not over $1,000,000 | $42,775, plus 9.75% of the excess above $500,000.    |
| Over $1,000,000                       | $91,525, plus 10.75% of the excess above $1,000,000. |
